# Journal: TCYB

Use for IEEE Transactions on Cybernetics manuscripts involving learning systems, pattern recognition, intelligent control, human-machine intelligence, domain generalization, multimodal representation learning, or cybernetic decision systems.

## Drafting Priorities

- Lead with the formal cybernetic or learning-control task, not broad application value.
- Emphasize closed-loop relevance, decision reliability, cross-domain generalization, and algorithmic principles when supported by evidence.
- Connect representation constraints to system-level behavior, such as target-domain risk, class separability, adaptation burden, or decision stability.
- Include complexity, inference path, and deployment constraints when the manuscript claims practical intelligent-system value.

## Reviewer Defense Checks

- The source-target or operating-condition shift is formalized.
- Algorithmic novelty is expressed as an objective, constraint, update rule, or theorem.
- Baselines include backbone-matched and domain-generalization or contrastive competitors when relevant.
- Claims about intelligent-system reliability are supported by OOD or stress-test evidence.
