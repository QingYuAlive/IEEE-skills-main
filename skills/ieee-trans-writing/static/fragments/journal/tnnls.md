# Journal: TNNLS

Use for IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems manuscripts involving neural architectures, learning theory, optimization, contrastive learning, invariant learning, robust training, uncertainty, or generalization.

## Drafting Priorities

- Present the learning problem, assumptions, objective, and algorithm before architectural prose.
- State theoretical results with assumptions, proof role, and relation to empirical validation.
- Define positive pairs, negative sets, domains, perturbations, or uncertainty variables before writing the corresponding loss.
- Include convergence, generalization, stability, or complexity analysis when the manuscript claims theoretical contribution.

## Reviewer Defense Checks

- Every architecture claim is backed by operator definitions and tensor shapes.
- Every objective term has a coefficient, tuning policy, and ablation.
- The theorem or bound does not overclaim beyond its assumptions.
- Experiments include strong learning-system baselines and sensitivity analysis for critical hyperparameters.
