# Journal: THMS

Use for IEEE Transactions on Human-Machine Systems polishing.

## Polishing Priorities

- Preserve subject, session, trial, force, gesture, sensor, and modality terminology.
- Make preprocessing choices explicit: filtering, normalization, windowing, overlap, sampling rate, channel handling, and trial treatment.
- Tie architecture language to signal morphology and deployment constraints.
- Bind cross-subject, cross-session, cross-force, and unseen-user claims to exact protocols.

## Reviewer-Risk Checks

- No subject-level or trial-level leakage is hidden by vague split language.
- Class imbalance, rest states, or gesture confusions are not masked by accuracy alone.
- Real-time or prosthetic-control claims include latency, throughput, or deployment constraints.
